Information

You are viewing a support request. The information provided by the author is shown in the left half of the content area, and a summary of the current state of the request is shown to the right. Comments added by all interested parties are shown at the bottom of the request.

Time differences are shown in terms of working hours according to your support package.

What do the statuses mean?
New: The request has just been created and has not yet been classified by an engineer.
Assigned: An engineer has picked up the request and is dealing with it.
Awaiting client: The request cannot be moved forward until more information regarding the request is available, or until a decision is made.
Deferred: No action is currently being taken on the request and further investigation has been postponed until a later date.
Scheduled for release: The engineer assigned has resolved the issue, but the code has not been published to the live servers yet. It may be assigned to another engineer to publish.
Complete: The engineer assigned has resolved the issue. The action taken still needs to be reviewed.
Closed: The issue is closed, and no further updates can be expected.
What do the classifications mean?
A: Usage Advice
B: Upgrade Advice
C: Reported Fault
D: Unreported Fault
E: Upgrade
CR: Change Request
OBS: Test observation
CFCG: Configuration change
What is the response time?
Response time is the time it takes us to classify your request and allocate somebody to deal with it. This is an important figure because we commit to responding within a certain period ranging from 15 minutes to 8 hours depending on your support package.
What is the resolution time?
The resolution (fix) time is the time it takes us to finish dealing with the request, that is, the time between the request being made and its status being set to completed.
